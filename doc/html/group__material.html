Parallel Material Point Method Simulator
material

Syntax

material(material-ID, rigid)
material(material-ID, linear, rho, E, nu, optional: damage-ID)
material(material-ID, neo-hookean, rho, E, nu, optional: damage-ID)
material(material-ID, eos-strength, eos-ID, strength-ID, optional: damage-ID, optional: temperature-ID)
  • material-ID: name of the material to be created.
  • rho: density of the material.
  • E: Young's modulus.
  • nu: Poisson's ration.
  • damage-ID: name of the damage law to be used (see Damage).
  • temperature-ID: name of the temperature law to be used (see Temperature).
  • eos-ID: name of EOS (Equation of State) to be used.
  • strength-ID: name of hardening law to be used (see Strength).

Examples

E = 115
nu = 0.31
rho = 8.94e-06
material(mat1, linear, rho, E, nu)

Defines a linear elastic material called 'mat1'. Its density is 8.94e-06, Poisson's ration 0.31 and Young's modulus 115.

Description

Four different kinds of materials are supported:

  • Rigid. The material does not deform.
  • Linear, defined by their density, Young's modulus \(E\) and Poisson's ratio \(\nu\). In this case, the stress is directly related to the strain increment:

    \begin{equation} \boldsymbol{\sigma} = 2G \Delta \boldsymbol{\varepsilon} + \lambda \text{tr}(\Delta \boldsymbol{\varepsilon}) \boldsymbol{I} \end{equation}

    where the shear modulus \(G\) and Lame parameter \(\lambda\) are obtained as follows:

    \begin{equation} G = \frac{E}{2 (1 + \nu)}\\ \lambda = \frac{E \nu}{(1 + \nu) (1 - 2 \nu)} \end{equation}

  • Neo-Hookean, defined by their density, Young's modulus \(E\) and Poisson's ratio \(\nu\). In this model, the first Piola-Kirchhoff stress tensor is given by:

    \begin{equation} \boldsymbol{P} = G (\boldsymbol{F} - \boldsymbol{F}^{-T}) + \lambda \left( \ln J \right)\boldsymbol{F}^{-T} \end{equation}

    where \(J\) is the determinant of \(\boldsymbol{F}\).

  • Elasto-plastic: defined by an equation of state (EOS) and a hardening law (Strength).

    For all non-rigid materials, damage (Damage) is supported, to add allow for the use of a damage law, its ID, damage-ID, needs to be given.

    Finally, for elasto-plastic materials, the increase of temperature coming from the plastic work can be talken into account using a temperature law (Temperature).
